# Academy Learning Guide: NES and SNES Map References

Use the records in `catalog.json` to compare player-facing spatial patterns. These are questions to investigate, not conclusions already established by the catalog.

## Overworlds and Regional Structure

- **The Legend of Zelda - Hyrule:** screen-scale landmark recurrence, route gating, and sparse environmental signals.
- **Zelda II - Hyrule:** macro-regions joined to towns, caves, and side-view transitions.
- **Final Fantasy II/IV - Overworld:** settlement spacing, coastline readability, and risk/relief cadence.
- **Chrono Trigger - 1000 A.D.:** compact world abstraction and destination hierarchy.
- **Super Mario RPG - World:** chapter-node abstraction as a useful contrast to continuous overworlds.

## Towns and Civic Spaces

- **Coneria, Pravoka, Town of Baron, Onett, Fourside, Potos, Mushroom Kingdom, Town of Belaine:** compare circulation spines, landmark placement, service clustering, edge treatment, and the relationship between critical destinations and optional space.

## Dungeons, Caves, and Castles

- **Level 1 (Eagle), Marsh Cave, Earth Cave, Parapa Palace, Palace of Dorago, Misty Cave, Guardia Castle, Cathedral, Giant Step, Pandora's Ruins, Kero Sewers, Cave of Baron:** compare branching factor, loop closure, floor/room identity, landmark intervals, visible goal cues, and optional detours.

## Tiles and Adjacency Vocabulary

- **A Link to the Past - Overworld Tiles:** inspect terrain-transition vocabulary and palette cohesion only. The source page includes a community warning that some transition tiles may be inaccurate, so this candidate must not be treated as authoritative adjacency evidence.

## Suggested Observation Labels

- `map_type`: `overworld`, `town`, `castle`, `dungeon`, `cave`, `hybrid`, or `tileset`
- `indoor_outdoor`: `indoor`, `outdoor`, or `mixed`
- `topology_questions`: `linear`, `branching`, `hub_and_spoke`, `loop_heavy`, `vertical`, or `chapter_nodes`
- `learning_scopes`: `landmark_spacing`, `route_readability`, `service_clustering`, `regional_gating`, `risk_reward`, `room_identity`, `terrain_adjacency`, or `negative_example`

Do not assign topology as fact from the title or thumbnail alone. Open the cited map, record an observation with exact provenance, and distinguish visible facts from interpretation.
